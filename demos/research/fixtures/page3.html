Cobalt Forge ships Forge 2.0 with faster cold starts and structured outputs

By Aisha Okafor - May 3, 2026

Forge 2.0 is the largest update to Cobalt Forge's managed inference platform since launch. Internal benchmarks on a 70B-parameter model show cold-start latency dropping from 2.4 s to 810 ms, p99 token latency improving 18%, and throughput rising 22% per GPU.

Structured outputs

Customers can pass a JSON schema with a request, and Forge constrains decoding so the response parses reliably. Tool-call schemas, enum constraints, and regex-typed fields are supported.

Multi-region failover

Forge 2.0 introduces region-aware routing. Requests fail over to a secondary region during capacity pressure or hardware events. Cobalt measured 99.97% availability over the last 90 days.

Pricing

There are no price changes. Structured-output mode is free during the May launch window and will be priced at a small premium afterward.
